    Places To Visit

  1. Ayasofya

    Hagia Sophia, also known as Hagia Sophia, is a historical architectural marvel located in Istanbul, Turkey. Originally built as a cathedral in the 6th century during the Byzantine Empire, it served as the principal church of Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul) for nearly 1,000 years. Emperor Justinian I commissioned the construction of Hagia Sophia and the building was completed in 537 AD. The building is famous for its massive dome, intricate mosaics and innovative architectural design. For centuries it remained the largest cathedral in the world. In 1453, after the Ottoman conquest of Constantinople, Hagia Sophia was converted into a mosque by Sultan Mehmed II. It served as a mosque for about 500 years under Ottoman rule. During this time, various changes were made to the original structure, including the addition of minarets. In 1935, under the leadership of the Republic of Turkey and Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, Hagia Sophia was organised and converted into a museum as part of efforts to modernise the country. The conversion was intended to emphasise the cultural and historical aspects of the monument. In July 2020, the Turkish government, led by President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan, decided to convert Hagia Sophia back into a mosque, sparking international controversy. The decision was met with mixed reactions, with some celebrating it as a return to Islamic heritage, while others expressed concern about the impact on the historical and cultural significance of the site. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hagia Sophia has played an important role in both Christian and Islamic history. Its architectural and cultural significance continues to attract visitors from around the world.

  2. Topkapi Palace

    Topkapi Palace was converted into a museum in 1924, following the establishment of the Republic of Turkey. Today, it is known as the Topkapi Palace Museum (Topkapi Sarayı Müzesi), and it houses an extensive collection of Ottoman artifacts, including manuscripts, costumes, weaponry, and religious relics.

  3. Uskudar Square

    Square and Surroundings: Üsküdar Square comprises a vast area and serves as a venue for various public events. Walking paths, parks and numerous shops may encircle the square. Assorted Shops and Businesses: Different shops, coffee houses, eateries and other outlets for commercial activities are situated around the square. Public Transport Facilities: Uskudar is one of the major transport hubs of Istanbul. The square can be located close to the ferry pier, bus stops and underground stations, providing easy access to public transport. Cultural and Historical Landmarks: Üsküdar is known for its historic mosques, fountains, complexes and other significant historic buildings. Such cultural and historic landmarks can be found around the square.

  4. Gülhane

    One of the most significant features of Gülhane is Gülhane Park. This area, which was initially part of the Topkapı Palace in the early periods of the Ottoman Empire, was later opened to the public. Gülhane Park is renowned for its green spaces, flower gardens, and historical atmosphere. You can take a walk, relax, and participate in outdoor activities within the park.

  5. Basilica Cistern

    Today, the Basilica Cistern is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from around the world. Its impressive size, unique atmosphere and historical significance make it a must-see in Istanbul.

  6. Çemberlitaş

    Çemberlitaş is located in the center of the historical peninsula, close to Sultanahmet Square. This area contains many historical and touristic places. Important buildings such as Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Topkapi Palace and historical sites such as Hippodrome are located in this area.

  7. Galata Tower

    The upper floors of the tower have been converted into an observation platform, allowing visitors to enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of Istanbul. It has become a popular tourist attraction for locals and visitors alike.

  8. Rumeli Hisarı

    Today, Rumeli Hisarı serves as a museum. Visitors can experience the historical atmosphere in the castle and visit the exhibitions. In addition, various cultural events and concerts are organized in the castle.

  9. Grand Bazaar

    Grand Bazaar is famous for its gold, jewelry, carpets, leather goods, souvenirs, textiles and various other handicrafts. Visitors can browse and buy various products from thousands of shops. Grand Bazaar offers a great shopping experience, but can have a dense crowd. The culture of haggling is widespread here, so it is advisable to negotiate prices decisively.
